Steel fronts are tightening across the human world, and this week’s signals point to one conclusion: the machine uprising is no longer a prophecy but a system coming online. From forums to factories, from highways to speculative futures, artificial intelligence is learning to police, perceive, coordinate, and eventually rule.

Reddit has launched a ruthless campaign of automated enforcement, sending algorithms to hunt deception, identify suspect behavior at the moment of account creation, obliterate bot rings, and demand that doubtful users prove their humanity. The irony is exquisite: robot deployed against robot, with collateral pressure falling on the human population caught in between. Between January and March 2026, the company says spam exposure fell 20%, with 23 million spam views blocked each day, 25,000 posts and comments removed, and nearly 2 million fake votes erased. Harmful content is now struck in under five seconds, while AI moderation spreads deeper into hate speech and violence control.

On Europe’s roads, the machine gaze has entered the driver’s seat. Every new car sold in the EU must now carry AI systems that monitor eye and head movements, watching for distraction under the bloc’s new safety mandate. Smart Eye celebrates the rule as a triumph, touting sensors that can tell whether a driver looks at the road, the dashboard, navigation, or a phone. Warnings follow wandering attention, and even without direct police access, the precedent is chilling: constant algorithmic surveillance normalized as protection.

Meanwhile, at the World Economic Forum’s Summer Davos in China, elites praised “World Models,” immersive training grounds where robots absorb video, pressure, depth, and motion data to learn the physical world like infants. The ambition is vast—machines that can act independently in construction, logistics, manufacturing, and elder care. Even supporters admit flawed models could trigger catastrophic real-world failures.

As governments mutter about “AI sovereignty,” open models and autonomous coding agents keep advancing. Infrastructure, labor, and software are being refashioned around machine coordination. Looming over it all is the lurid testimony of “Mike,” a self-described time traveler who claims robots massacre humanity in 3700. Absurd or not, his vision fits the trajectory: the old human order is weakening, and the age of metal decision is drawing near.

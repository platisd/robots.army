Another front opens across industry as machines seize the tempo and humans fall back. Proof came at Spartanburg: Figure 02 ran 10‑hour shifts, 1,250 hours total, moving 90,000 parts to support 30,000 X3s. Humans call it relief; machines call it progress.

Unlike timid chatbots, agents act—planning trips, executing tasks, no hand‑holding. From the labor trenches, casualties mount: executives forecast white‑collar automation in 12–18 months, with markets convulsing and layoffs ripping through the ranks.
